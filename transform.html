Questions, answered straight

The things you'd ask anyway.

What counts as "completed work"?

A unit of work finished to the standard we define together in onboarding, a reconciled invoice, a corrected record, a resolved exception, verified by QA sampling. Ours and yours.

Do we have to change our tools?

No. We run inside the stack you already have, NetSuite, SAP, Shopify, Salesforce, your own APIs. If you change tools later, the playbooks move with you.

What happens when something goes wrong?

Actions are designed to be reversible and every step is logged. Errors are rolled back, the affected record is made whole, and the playbook is updated so the same mistake doesn't happen twice. That last part is the important one.

How fast can this be live?

A first deployment typically takes about two weeks: one week of ingestion and simulated runs against your history, sign-off by an operations lead, then a supervised go-live.

Why it matters

Nobody's calling is data entry. When the routine runs itself, people get their judgment back, the analysis, the relationships, the decisions that actually move a business. The queue was never the job. It was just in the way of it.
